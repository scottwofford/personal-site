The 5 Choices to Extraordinary Productivity

A framework from FranklinCovey for making high-impact decisions about your time.

Overview

The 5 Choices framework builds on time management fundamentals (like Eisenhower) but adds decision-making clarity and attention management for the modern workplace.

The 5 Choices

  1. Act on the Important, Don't React to the Urgent
  2. Go for Extraordinary, Don't Settle for Ordinary
  3. Schedule the Big Rocks, Don't Sort Gravel
  4. Rule Your Technology, Don't Let It Rule You
  5. Fuel Your Fire, Don't Burn Out

Key Concepts

Big Rocks

Your most important priorities. If you schedule these first, everything else fits around them. If you fill your calendar with gravel (small tasks), the big rocks won't fit.

Q2 (Quadrant 2) Living

Similar to Eisenhower's "Important but Not Urgent" quadrant—this is where strategic, high-impact work lives.

30/10 Promise

Spend 30 minutes each week planning, and you'll save 10+ hours of reactive scrambling.
