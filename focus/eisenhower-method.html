The Eisenhower Method

Time management equals prioritization.

The Eisenhower Matrix (also called the Urgent-Important Matrix) is a decision-making framework that helps you prioritize tasks by urgency and importance.

The Matrix

UrgentNot Urgent
ImportantDo FirstSchedule
Not ImportantDelegateEliminate

The Key Insight

The exercise isn't just about sorting tasks—it's about understanding that growing your impact over time means doing more things that are important but not urgent.

Most people spend too much time in the "urgent" columns, reacting to demands rather than proactively building toward goals.

Quadrant Breakdown

  1. Do First (Urgent + Important): Crises, deadlines, emergencies. Handle these immediately.
  2. Schedule (Not Urgent + Important): Strategic planning, relationship building, learning, prevention. This is where long-term success is built. Block time for these.
  3. Delegate (Urgent + Not Important): Interruptions, some meetings, some emails. Others can often handle these.
  4. Eliminate (Not Urgent + Not Important): Time wasters, busywork, some emails. Cut these ruthlessly.

Practical Application

Ask yourself regularly:

  • What am I doing that feels urgent but isn't actually important?
  • What important things am I neglecting because they're not screaming at me?
  • How can I create systems to spend more time in Quadrant 2?
"What is important is seldom urgent and what is urgent is seldom important." — Dwight D. Eisenhower
