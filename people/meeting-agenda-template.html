Meeting Agenda Template

A simple structure for any meeting.

Template

### [Date] - [Meeting Title]

**Desired Outcome:** What decision needs to be made?
What should be true after this meeting that isn't true now?

**Attendees:** List required attendees

---

#### Agenda
1. Item 1
2. Item 2
3. Item 3

#### Notes
1. Capture discussion points

#### Action Items
1. [Action] - Owner: [Name], Due: [Date]

Why This Structure Works

Desired Outcome

Forces you to think: Why are we meeting? If you can't articulate the outcome, maybe you don't need the meeting.

Agenda (Before)

  • Shared in advance so attendees can prepare
  • Ordered by priority (most important first)
  • Time-boxed if needed

Notes (During)

  • Capture key points, not transcripts
  • Note decisions explicitly
  • Project the doc if possible so everyone sees the same thing

Action Items (After)

  • Every action needs an owner and a date
  • Send summary within 24 hours
  • Follow up. The meeting organizer owns this

Meeting Types

Decision Meeting

  • Outcome: A specific decision
  • Prep: Decision doc with options and recommendation
  • Output: Documented decision + rationale

Status Meeting

  • Outcome: Alignment on progress and blockers
  • Prep: Status updates in a shared doc (read before meeting)
  • Output: Decisions on blockers, updated action items

Brainstorm Meeting

  • Outcome: Ideas generated (not decisions)
  • Prep: Problem statement shared in advance
  • Output: List of ideas to evaluate later

1:1 Meeting

  • Outcome: Varies: coaching, alignment, relationship
  • Prep: Both parties bring topics
  • Output: Actions, decisions, or just connection

Keep meetings short. Keep agendas tight. Respect people's time.
