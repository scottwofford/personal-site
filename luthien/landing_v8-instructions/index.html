Luthien QA Trial

User onboarding test — walk through our product as a new user and tell us what's compelling, what's confusing, and what's broken.

Goal: Follow the onboarding flow end-to-end. Note what sounds interesting or exciting, what's confusing, and what's broken. Be brutally honest — we want real reactions, not polished feedback.

Part 0: Intro Call ~15 min

Before we start, we'll do a quick screen-share call. This helps us understand your background so we can interpret your feedback better. We'll ask you to share your screen and walk us through:

  1. Your typical day: Walk me through a typical coding or ops day for you.
  2. Where AI shows up: Where does AI (Cursor, Claude Code, Copilot, other LLMs) show up in that flow? What motivates you to reach for AI versus doing it manually?
  3. Code review: If you have teammates or AI coding assistants, how do you handle code reviews — who signs off?
  4. Frustrations: Think of the last time an AI assistant was really frustrating. What happened? What specifically was the most frustrating part?
  5. Worst case: What keeps you up at night about AI in your workflow? Any security worries, spending concerns, or privacy risks?
  6. Workarounds: What do you currently do to cope with AI failure modes? If you had a magic wand to prevent one AI failure, what would it do?

Part 0b: Start Recording ~2 min

Before starting the test — set up two things:
  • Screen recording (Loom, Zoom, QuickTime, OBS) — capture your screen, webcam, and audio
  • AI note-taker (SuperWhisper, Otter.ai, or similar) — so we get a transcript of your think-aloud, not just the video

Think out loud as you go. Say what you're thinking, what you expect, what surprises you. The recording + transcript are as valuable as the written report — they give us a real-time window into your brain.

Part 2: README Quick Start ~45–60 min

README.md (PR #179 branch)

2a. Read the intro sections ~5 min

Before installing anything, read from the top of the README. Note what clicks immediately, what's confusing, and whether you'd keep reading.

2b. Follow the Quick Start exactly ~30–45 min

Follow every step exactly as written. Do NOT skip ahead or improvise. The point is to test whether a new user can follow these instructions cold.

For each step, note: Did it work as written? If not — exact error + screenshot. Anything confusing? Anything that felt satisfying or well-designed? How long did it take?

Part 3: Your Report

Create a Google Doc or markdown file with:

  1. Landing page feedback (Part 1) — what worked, what didn't
  2. README feedback (Part 2a + 2b) — clarity, setup issues
  3. Functionality feedback (Part 2c) — did it actually work?
  4. Overall impressions:
    • How long did the whole thing take?
    • On a scale of 1–5, how easy was it to get running?
    • What was the most compelling part?
    • What was the most confusing part?
    • What would you change first?

Send the report to scott@luthienresearch.org.

Tips:
  • We want the good, the bad, and the ugly. If something is broken or confusing, that's valuable feedback — not a failure on your part. Equally, if something clicks or feels well-designed, say so.
  • Don't fix things yourself. Just document what you see.
  • If stuck on a step for >10 min, note where you got stuck, skip it, and keep going.
  • Docker builds images locally on first run — can be slow (~5–10 min). That's expected.
