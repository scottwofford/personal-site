X: Somebody is wrong on the internet

Community Notes, mostly in Spanish.

What I Actually Do on X

I'm not much of a poster. My main contribution is Community Notes—the crowdsourced fact-checking system that adds context to misleading posts.

Interestingly, most of the notes I write and rate are in Spanish. There seems to be a supply gap: not enough Community Notes contributors who speak Spanish, relative to the amount of Spanish-language misinformation that needs context.

Why This Matters

Community Notes has a clever design: notes only appear if they get agreement from people across the political spectrum. This "bridging" algorithm means notes that do appear tend to be genuinely informative rather than partisan dunks.

The Spanish-language gap means misinformation can spread longer before getting context. Every note I write or rate helps close that gap a little.

The Bigger Picture

This is part of my broader interest in fixing wrong information at scale. Community Notes, Wikipedia editing, and eventually AI-assisted verification—they're all different tools for the same goal: making the information ecosystem more accurate.
